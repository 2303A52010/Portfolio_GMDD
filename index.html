02 · About Me

Who Am I?

I'm Macharla Goutham, a third-year Computer Science undergraduate at SR University, Warangal. This portfolio showcases my work for the Game Mechanics subject — a hands-on exploration of game design and development using Unity.

Through this subject, I've built 9 individual Unity games — each focused on a distinct mechanic: movement, physics, AI logic, puzzle systems, input handling, and more. Every project is a step deeper into understanding what makes games feel alive.

I also led a team project, the 3D Endless Runner — where I coordinated a team of 6 developers to bring a fully realized 3D experience to life. Game development, for me, is not just code — it's the craft of engineering emotion through interaction.

05 · Team Project

3D Endless Runner

3D ENDLESS RUNNER
TEAM PROJECT · 6 MEMBERS · UNITY 3D
3D Endless Runner
Create a 3D endless runner that combines spatial navigation, strategic obstacle management, and dynamic world generation to maintain player engagement indefinitely. The focus is on procedural environment design, player progression, and sensory immersion.

As team lead, I coordinated 6 developers to architect a world that never ends — procedurally spawning obstacles, collectibles, and environmental segments in real-time. The game features adaptive difficulty scaling, a layered audio system, and a polished visual progression that rewards skilled players.

06 · Mentor

My Faculty

👨‍🏫
Dr. Vijaya Chandra Jadala
Professor of Game Mechanics · SR University, Warangal
in LinkedIn Profile
Under the guidance of Dr. Jadala, I learned to approach game development not just as coding, but as the craft of designing experiences — each mechanic a deliberate choice that shapes how a player thinks and feels.

His teaching philosophy bridges the gap between academic theory and hands-on implementation — pushing us to question why a mechanic works before writing a single line of code. That approach transformed how I think about every system I build.

Dr. Jadala challenged our team to think beyond the screen — to consider player psychology, feedback loops, and the invisible design choices that make a game feel inevitable rather than accidental.
